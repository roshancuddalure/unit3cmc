# Logbook Progression Review Pattern

## Purpose

Use this when the logbook needs to support chief/faculty review, progression discussion, or print-friendly reporting instead of only data capture.

## Recommended review outputs

- period summary with total cases
- direct-care ratio
- breadth across domains
- procedure-category mix
- emergency exposure
- complex-case exposure
- independent-with-review exposure
- strengths inferred from recent activity
- watch points that can guide supervision

## Why this matters

Supervisors need more than a case count. Progression discussions become much better when the UI highlights whether a person is widening breadth, moving from observation to direct care, and encountering emergency or complex work.

## Print/export rule

- Prefer a print-friendly HTML report first.
- Keep it de-identified.
- Include the analysis narrative, key metrics, and case register for the selected period.
- Avoid exporting hidden patient references or raw internal IDs.

## Taxonomy rule

- Add procedure-category structure when the specialty needs finer analysis than broad clinical domains alone.
- Keep the category list controlled and clinically meaningful.
- Do not replace the actual procedure title; use both.
